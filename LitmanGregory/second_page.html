Fund Selection

Rigorous, intensive due diligence process in evaluating manager

Over the years we have created several pieces that describe our fund manager due diligence process—these are listed below. Each is presented somewhat differently, but individually or together they will help our subscribers gain a solid understanding of the rigorous, intensive due diligence process we go through in evaluating managers.

Fundamentals of Our Due Diligence Process

Our due diligence process is too detailed and complex to lend itself well to a simple “flow chart,” but this is what we had in mind when we created the following presentation. It is designed to provide an in-depth and generally sequential examination of our due diligence process. The numbered items below make some prerequisite points about our process and its goals. Clicking the “next” button then takes you in to the first of the eight broad steps in the process.

  1. The effectiveness of our due diligence process is a function of our experience (knowing what to look for and how to find it), our commitment to being thorough, and our very high standards. Without high standards, thoroughness does not matter.
  2. We are willing to miss out on some good funds and be methodical in our analysis in order to increase our batting average. We believe the quality of our recommended funds is more important than the quantity. Theoretically, if all of the funds we recommend are winners, missing a few other winners won’t matter. (This does not imply that we expect to always be right—we make this statement only to clarify our point.)
  3. Our due diligence process is an evolving one. We try to improve on our current process by constantly re-evaluating how we do things. Our focus goes beyond meeting our fiduciary responsibility; we aim to improve our ability to identify managers who will excel. The following presentation serves as a detailed snapshot of our process as it exists now; we will continue to refine it as we learn from various due diligence experiences.
How We Allocate Resources

Our research priorities are a function of the high-priority issues and projects that come up (see bullet points below) and the ongoing monitoring we do for all of our Recommended funds (see bullet points below left).

Ideas for new funds to research come from:

  • Conducting performance screens using fund and separate account databases
  • Following up on new or existing funds from families we already know and respect
    • Researching new funds for a tactical allocation we want to make in the model portfolios
    • Talking to industry contacts and reading investment publications
    • Talking with fund managers we know and respect to
    • learn about other managers they regard highly

After prioritizing research projects, each fund is assigned a primary and a secondary analyst who participate in the calls and the site visit.
